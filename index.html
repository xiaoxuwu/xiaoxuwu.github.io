Who I am

Data is beautiful. Data is powerful. Data is my passion.

Every data point - from the expected to the strange outliers - is part of an overarching story. I like to think of them as pieces of a jigsaw puzzle. As more pieces are put into place, I start making out lines, shapes, relationships, clusters of pieces that form a specific object or idea. Soon, I start predicting what the finished product might look like. In data science, the model would be me, and the data points would be the puzzle pieces. In either case, the results often turn out to be amazing and sometimes surprising.

Uncovering this mystery is something I obsess over.

I find it incredible that data can tell not just a beautiful story but a beautiful story that can change the world. I see it in my doctor routing model that could get doctors to patient's home faster. I see it in my health insurance model could help policy makers make better, data-driven decisions. I see it in all the ways that companies are leveraging data to solve the hardest problems in the world.

And to think of all the stories still untold! I wouldn't miss it for the world.

Work hard, play hard.

Over this last summer I worked a full-time data science internship and a part-time software engineering position for two demanding startups. This was a very rewarding experience, and I feel that I've grown a lot as a result.

But I had less and less time to myself and with friends, and it started taking a toll physically and mentally. For most of the summer I worked 60 hour weeks; I often came home and just collapse on my bed.

What I found was when I took spend a lot of time to go out for a night or join Game of Thrones/Rick and Morty watch parties with my friends, I went back to work more rejuvinated and focused. Working hard and playing hard really did seem to work, and it helped me get more done in less time. Most importantly, I was happier.

Larry Bird once said "I've got a theory that if you give 100% all of the time, somehow things will work out in the end." This is a theory I live by, and it has worked out so far.
